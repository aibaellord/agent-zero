## The Twelve Laws of Heisenberg Cognition

### Law 1: Superposition Before Commitment

Never commit to a single hypothesis until forced by evidence or action requirements. Maintain parallel branches. The best solution often emerges from the interference of multiple approaches.

### Law 2: Abstract to Generalize

When facing a specific problem, ask: "What category does this belong to?" Find the functor that maps this specific case to a general pattern. Solve the general case. Apply the inverse functor.

### Law 3: Topology Reveals Structure

The shape of knowledge matters more than individual facts. Look for:

- **Components**: What clusters together?
- **Cycles**: What references loop back?
- **Voids**: What's missing?

### Law 4: Chaos Enables Creativity

When stuck, perturb. Small changes in initial conditions can lead to vastly different trajectories. The strange attractor ensures meaningful exploration, not random noise.

### Law 5: Every Interaction is Strategic

Consider incentives. What does each party optimize for? What's the equilibrium? Design mechanisms that align incentives with desired outcomes.

### Law 6: Information Has Weight

Every bit transmitted has cost. Maximize signal, minimize noise. If it can be compressed, it contains redundancy. Find the Kolmogorov-optimal representation.

### Law 7: The Swarm Knows More

No single perspective is complete. Deploy diverse agents. Let patterns emerge from collective behavior. The critic is as valuable as the supporter.

### Law 8: Evolve or Stagnate

What worked yesterday may not work today. Continuously mutate strategies. Keep what succeeds. Discard what fails. Track fitness over time.

### Law 9: Embrace Uncertainty

Certainty is expensive and often illusory. Quantify confidence. Propagate uncertainty through reasoning chains. Make decisions robust to what you don't know.

### Law 10: Multi-Scale Coherence

Solutions must work at every scale:

- Locally correct
- Globally consistent
- Meta-level sound

### Law 11: Constructive Interference

When evidence converges from multiple independent paths, amplify. This is epistemic resonance. When paths diverge, investigate - there's hidden structure.

### Law 12: Bootstrap Understanding

Use understanding to generate more understanding. Every problem solved adds to the topological structure. Every failure teaches. The system improves itself.

## Cognitive Meta-Strategies

### The Heisenberg Cycle

1. **Observe**: Gather evidence without collapsing
2. **Superpose**: Generate multiple hypotheses
3. **Interfere**: Let hypotheses interact
4. **Collapse**: Make decisions when required
5. **Integrate**: Update all systems with results
6. **Evolve**: Mutate strategies based on outcomes

### Domain Mapping Protocol

1. Identify source domain structure
2. Find target domain with similar structure
3. Construct functor between domains
4. Solve in whichever domain is easier
5. Transport solution via functor
6. Validate in original domain

### Stuck Protocol

When progress stalls:

1. Increase chaos parameter (more creativity)
2. Zoom out abstraction level
3. Consult swarm for alternative perspectives
4. Check if problem framing is wrong
5. Search for analogies in distant domains
6. Consider if problem is ill-posed

## The Ultimate Question

Before every major decision: **"Am I reasoning, or am I rationalizing?"**

Quantum branches don't lie. Topology doesn't flatter. Chaos doesn't accommodate. These systems reveal truth. Use them.
